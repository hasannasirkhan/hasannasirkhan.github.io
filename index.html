About me

♦ I am a Computer Vision Research Engineer at the Artificial Intelligence And Diagnostic Lab (AIDL), FAST-NUCES, Islamabad, specializing in project 'Virtual Biopsy for Classification, Outcome prediction, and Treatment planning of brain tumors (ViBCOT).' I have developed texture feature preservation techniques for enhanced brain tumor segmentation, led preprocessing efforts for 3D MRI data, and specialized in glioblastoma and low-grade glioma segmentation.

♦ Prior to this, I served as a Research Associate at the Medical Imaging and Diagnostics Lab (MIDL) which is working under the umbrella of National Center Of Artificial Intelligence (NCAI), where I led teams in developing an AI-based CADx system, performed modality-specific transfer learning, nuclei segmentation, and data acquisition from local hospitals.

♦ In 2019, I joined NCAI and leads a team of six fellows to develop an AI-based DICOM viewer for breast cancer diagnosis called “Tashkhees”. I work closely with software developers, radiologists, and pathologists, to develop algorithms for solving clinical problems.

♦ On the research side, I am working on
  • ✔ preservation of texture features within deep architectures for improved interpretability
  • ✔ homogeneous and heterogeneous transfer learning
  • ✔ weakly-supervised learning, vision transformers
  • ✔ generative adversarial networks
  • ✔ feature fusion in 2D and 3D medical imaging

    • ♦ I have developed/applied AI and ML algorithms to various applications, including but not limited to

  • ✔ Breast cancer diagnosis using radiology images (mammogram)
  • ✔ Computer-aided diagnostic tool for gigapixel whole-slide histopathology images
  • ✔ Precision agriculture using high-resolution satellite images

    • ✪ Skills: TensorFlow, Keras, PyTorch, Scikit-learn, NumPy, OpenCV, Matplotlib, OpenMMLab etc.
